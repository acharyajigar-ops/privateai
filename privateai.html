The Problem

US regulated professions face federal & state AI liability

HIPAA, ABA Model Rules, SEC regulations, and state bar ethics opinions all create specific legal obligations around client and patient data. Cloud AI platforms were not built with these obligations in mind.

01

HIPAA & Protected Health Information

Using cloud AI to process PHI without a signed BAA is a direct HIPAA violation. Most AI platforms either don't offer BAAs or offer terms that don't adequately protect PHI from training data use.

02

Attorney-Client Privilege & ABA Ethics

ABA Model Rule 1.6 requires attorneys to make reasonable efforts to prevent unauthorized disclosure of client information. Multiple state bar opinions have flagged that cloud AI on client matter risks breaching this duty.

03

SEC, FINRA & Investment Adviser Rules

SEC Regulation S-P and FINRA Rule 4370 require safeguarding non-public customer information. Using unvetted AI tools on client financial data creates regulatory exposure compliance teams aren't adequately addressing.

04

CCPA & State Privacy Laws

California, Virginia, Colorado, and a growing list of states have enacted comprehensive privacy laws. Sending client data to cloud AI creates processing relationships that may require consent firms haven't secured.
