The Problem

UK regulated professions face a specific AI compliance risk

The SRA, ICO, FCA, and CQC all have clear expectations around client data. Cloud AI tools were not designed with UK professional regulation in mind — and using them on client data creates real exposure.

01

SRA & Solicitor-Client Privilege

The SRA has issued explicit AI guidance. Feeding client communications to a third-party AI platform risks breaching confidentiality obligations and may constitute a waiver of legal privilege under UK law.

02

UK GDPR & ICO Compliance

Sending personal data to OpenAI or Microsoft requires a lawful basis and a valid Data Processing Agreement. Without these, every use of cloud AI on client data is a potential ICO-reportable breach under UK GDPR Article 33.

03

FCA Data Security Obligations

FCA-regulated firms must ensure client data is protected under SYSC 8. Using unauthorised AI tools introduces uncontrolled third-party data processing outside your approved vendor framework.

04

Shadow IT in Small Practices

You cannot stop fee earners using free AI tools on personal devices. The only effective response is to provide a compliant, practice-wide alternative that is genuinely better than what they are currently using.
