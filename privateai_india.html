The Problem

Indian regulated professions face serious AI data risks

India's DPDPA 2023 creates new obligations for data fiduciaries. BCI, ICAI, SEBI, NMC, and RCI all have professional conduct rules directly implicated when client data is fed into foreign cloud AI platforms.

01

DPDPA 2023 & Cross-Border Data Transfer

India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 imposes obligations on data fiduciaries. When a lawyer, CA, or doctor uses cloud AI on client data, that personal data is transmitted to foreign servers — creating cross-border transfer risk and potential penalties under the Act.

02

Advocate-Client Privilege & BCI Ethics

Bar Council of India Rules of Professional Conduct require advocates to maintain strict confidentiality. Uploading case briefs and legal strategy to a foreign AI platform directly undermines this duty and risks privilege under the Indian Evidence Act 1872.

03

SEBI, IRDAI & Financial Data

SEBI's cybersecurity frameworks and IRDAI's data protection guidelines require regulated entities to maintain controls over client financial data. Using unvetted foreign AI tools on investor or policyholder data creates significant regulatory exposure.

04

IT Act / SPDI Rules & Sensitive Data

The IT (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules 2011 define Sensitive Personal Data or Information — including health, financial, and biometric data. Transmitting SPDI to a foreign AI service without appropriate contracts and consent is a clear violation.
